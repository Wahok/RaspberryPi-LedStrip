Tutorial: Connect a Raspberry Pi with a LED-Strip

In this short tutorial I will show you how to connect a RGB LED-Strip to your Raspberry Pi.

Attention: I'm not responsible for any damage to your Strip or Raspberry Pi. Do this at your own risk!

What do you need?

  • Your RGB LED Strip (with 3 pins for RGB and 1 pin for 12V DC)
  • Any breadboard
  • Jumper Wires Male to Female
  • Jumper Wires Male to Male
  • Three MOSFETs
    • For example: IRLZ34N
  • Power Adapter (12V DC ~2A)
  • Extra-low voltage connector
    • Has to fit with your Power Adapter
    • I use one with screws for a easy connecting with the wires
    • If you already have a controller, you can also desolder the Extra-low voltage connector from there and solder it up with two wires
  • The software pi-blaster on your Raspberry Pi

1. Connect the MOSFETs

  • If you look at the front of the MOSFETs:
    • The first pin from left is the Gate
    • The second one the Drain
    • The third one the Source
  • Just put the three MOSFETs onto your breadboard
  • Connect the Source pins with the ground bus of your breadboard

2. Connect the LED-Strip

  • Connect for each color the Drain pin with the associated color of the LED-Strip
  • Put a wire from the 12V pin of the LED-Strip to the supply voltage bus of the breadboard

3. Connect the Raspberry Pi

Image source raspberrypi-spy.co.uk

  • Connect one of the ground pins with the ground bus of the breadboard
  • Connect for each color one of the green pins with the Gate of the MOSFETs
    • I will use:
      • GPIO17 for Red
      • GPIO22 for Green
      • GPIO24 for Blue

4. Connect the Extra-low voltage connector

  • If you have one with Screws:
    • Fasten one wire with the ground and one withe the voltage part in place
  • Connect the ground wire with the ground bus of the breadboard
  • Connect the voltage wire with the voltage bus of the breadboard
  • Connect your Power Adapter with the Extra-low voltage connector
  • Plug in your Power Adapter

5. Testing

  • Connect to your Raspberry Pi via SSH or Terminal
  • Type into your console:
    • echo "17=1" > /dev/pi-blaster and your LED should be red
    • echo "22=0.5" > /dev/pi-blaster and your LED should be yellow
    • echo "24=0.5" > /dev/pi-blaster and your LED should be purple

What now?

  • Now you can do everything with your LED-Strip
    • For example you can use python to control the LED-Strip
    • With os.system("echo PIN=BRIGHTNESS > /dev/pi-blaster") you can echo to /dev/pi-blaster
    • You can also try my fading script: http://fading
  • If you have questions, feel free to ask!
